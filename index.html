About World Language Day

World Language Day is a celebration of the incredible diversity of languages spoken across the globe. Languages are not just tools for communication; they are vessels of culture, history, and identity. This day reminds us of the importance of preserving linguistic heritage and fostering understanding across cultures.

From ancient scripts to modern dialects, every language tells a story. Join us in exploring the rich tapestry of human expression!

Exploring Ancient Scripts

Cuneiform: The Birth of Writing

Cuneiform is one of the earliest known writing systems, originating in Mesopotamia around 3400 BCE. It was written on clay tablets using a reed stylus, creating wedge-shaped marks that gave it its name (from the Latin "cuneus," meaning "wedge").

Brahmi: The Root of Many Asian Scripts

The Brahmi script is the ancestor of many scripts used in South and Southeast Asia. It dates back to the 3rd century BCE and was used to write a variety of languages, including Sanskrit and Prakrit.

Malay Manuscripts: A Glimpse into Southeast Asian History

Malay manuscripts are a treasure trove of history, literature, and culture. Written in the Jawi script (an Arabic-based alphabet), these manuscripts provide insights into the rich heritage of the Malay world.

The ancient map : The Celtic Alphabet

Ogham is an ancient Celtic script used primarily in Ireland and Britain. It consists of vertical and diagonal lines inscribed on stone monuments, often used for memorials and boundary markers.
